*Income Checker - Prospect Research & Lead Generation*
*Last updated: 2026-01-28*

## Overview

This document provides step-by-step instructions for building targeted prospect lists of property managers and landlords. Our primary targets are:

- Small property management companies (10-50 units)
- Solo landlords with 5+ properties
- Real estate investors with rental portfolios

**Goal:** Build a qualified list of 500+ contacts in Month 1, growing to 2,000+ by Month 3.

### 1.1 Google Maps Scraping

**Yield:** 50-150 contacts per city
**Time:** 30-45 minutes per city

#### Step-by-Step Process

1. **Open Google Maps** (maps.google.com)

2. **Search using these queries:**
   - `"property management" [city, state]`
   - `"property manager" [city, state]`
   - `"rental management" [city, state]`
   - `"landlord services" [city, state]`
   - `"apartment management" [city, state]`

3. **For each result, capture:**
   - Business name
   - Phone number

4. **Visit each website to find:**
   - Owner/manager name (check "About Us" or "Team" page)

### 1.5 Craigslist Rental Listings (Trace to PM)

**Yield:** 20-50 unique PMs per city per week
**Time:** 45 minutes per city

#### Step-by-Step Process

1. **Go to:** `[city].craigslist.org/search/apa` (apartments/housing for rent)

2. **Apply filters:**
   - Posted by: Owner (will include small landlords and PMs)
   - Min price: $800+ (filters out spam/scams)
   - Has image: Yes

3. **Identify property manager listings by looking for:**
   - Multiple listings from same phone number
   - Company name mentioned
   - Professional-looking photos
   - "Managed by..." language
   - Application fees mentioned (indicates professional operation)

4. **For each PM identified:**
   - Search their company name
   - Find website and contact info
   - Note number of active listings (proxy for portfolio size)

5. **Track unique phone numbers** - Same number across multiple listings = PM or multi-property landlord

#### Craigslist Search Tips

```
Search for professional phrases:
- "property management"
- "managed by"
- "application required"
- "professional landlord"
- "management company"
```

### 1.8 County Property Records (Finding Multi-Property Landlords)

**Yield:** 50-200 multi-property owners per county
**Time:** 2-3 hours per county

#### Step-by-Step Process

1. **Find your county assessor/recorder website:**
   - Google: "[County name] property records" or "[County] assessor parcel search"

2. **Michigan County Portals:**

   | County | Portal URL |
   |--------|-----------|
   | Wayne (Detroit) | `waynecounty.com/departments/treasurer/property-search.aspx` |
   | Kent (Grand Rapids) | `accesskent.com/Property` |
   | Washtenaw (Ann Arbor) | `ewashtenaw.org/government/departments/equalization` |
   | Oakland | `oakgov.com/advantagetax` |
   | Ingham (Lansing) | `ingham.org/treasurer` |

3. **Search strategy for multi-property owners:**

   **Method A: Address Pattern Search**
   - Search for addresses in known rental-heavy areas
   - Note owner names that appear multiple times
   - Build list of repeat owners

   **Method B: Owner Name Search**
   - Search common LLC patterns: "[Name] Properties LLC", "[Name] Rentals"
   - Search: "Property Management" in owner name field
   - Note addresses and cross-reference ownership

   **Method C: Mailing Address Analysis**
   - Owners with mailing addresses different from property addresses are often investors
   - Same mailing address on multiple properties = multi-property landlord

4. **For each multi-property owner identified:**
   - Count total properties owned
   - Note property types (SFH vs multi-family)
   - Search owner name + "property management" or + "rentals" to find company info
   - Use skip tracing (see Section 2.3) to find contact info

#### Prioritization Criteria

- **High priority:** 10+ properties, single owner/LLC name
- **Medium priority:** 5-9 properties
- **Low priority:** 2-4 properties (may be accidental landlords)

### 2.3 Skip Tracing Services (Finding Landlord Contact Info)

**Use case:** When you have property owner name from county records but no contact info.

| Service | Cost | Best For |
|---------|------|----------|
| **Skip Genie** | $0.15-0.20/record | High-volume skip tracing, real estate focused |
| **BatchSkipTracing** | $0.12-0.18/record | Bulk uploads, good for county record lists |
| **PropStream** | $99/mo | Property data + owner info + skip tracing |
| **TLOxp** | $50/mo + per search | Deep skip traces, professional investigators |
| **BeenVerified Pro** | $27/mo | Basic contact finding, good for small batches |

#### Skip Tracing Process

1. **Prepare your list:**
   - Owner name (first, last)
   - Property address
   - Mailing address (if different)
   - Any known contact info

2. **Upload to skip tracing service**

3. **Receive back:**
   - Phone numbers (mobile preferred)
   - Email addresses
   - Alternative addresses
   - Associated businesses

4. **Verify and clean:**
   - Remove disconnected numbers
   - Validate email deliverability
   - Remove deceased/wrong person matches

#### Recommended Workflow

1. Pull multi-property owner list from county records (free)
2. Filter to 5+ properties (your ICP)
3. Run through BatchSkipTracing (~$0.15/record)
4. Add to Apollo or CRM for outreach
5. Budget: ~$50-100 for 300-500 skip traces

### 3.3 Data Hygiene Practices

#### Weekly Hygiene Tasks (30 min/week)

1. **Deduplicate:**
   - Sort by company name, look for variations
   - Sort by email, remove exact duplicates
   - Merge contacts at same company

2. **Validate Emails:**
   - Use NeverBounce or ZeroBounce for bulk validation ($0.003-0.01/email)
   - Remove bounced emails immediately
   - Mark "catch-all" domains for manual verification

3. **Update Statuses:**
   - Mark unsubscribes as "Do Not Contact"
   - Update contact info from reply signatures
   - Note company changes (mergers, closures)

#### Monthly Hygiene Tasks (2 hours/month)

1. **Re-verify:**
   - Run email verification on full list
   - Check for job changes on LinkedIn
   - Update phone numbers that went bad

2. **Enrich:**
   - Add missing Units Managed estimates
   - Research company websites for intel
   - Update lead scores based on engagement

3. **Archive:**
   - Move 6-month non-responders to archive
   - Remove closed companies
   - Clean up test records

## Part 4: Prioritization Framework

### 4.1 Lead Scoring Model

**Score each lead 1-5 using this rubric:**

#### Company Size Score (40% weight)

| Units Managed | Score |
|---------------|-------|
| 50+ | 5 |
| 31-50 | 5 |
| 10-30 | 4 |
| 5-9 | 2 |
| 1-4 | 1 |

#### Role Score (30% weight)

| Title | Score |
|-------|-------|
| Owner/CEO/President | 5 |
| Operations Manager/Director | 4 |
| Property Manager (Senior) | 3 |
| Property Manager | 2 |
| Leasing Agent/Assistant | 1 |

#### Engagement Signals Score (20% weight)

| Signal | Score |
|--------|-------|
| Replied positively | 5 |
| Opened 3+ emails | 4 |
| Visited website | 4 |
| Opened 1-2 emails | 2 |
| No engagement | 1 |

#### Fit Signals Score (10% weight)

| Signal | Score |
|--------|-------|
| Mentioned screening pain | 5 |
| Association member | 4 |
| Professional website | 3 |
| Limited online presence | 2 |
| Red flags (bad reviews, legal issues) | 1 |

#### Composite Score Calculation

```
Lead Score = (Size Score × 0.4) + (Role Score × 0.3) + (Engagement Score × 0.2) + (Fit Score × 0.1)
```

**Priority Tiers:**
- **Tier 1 (Score 4.0+):** Contact within 24 hours
- **Tier 2 (Score 3.0-3.9):** Contact within 48 hours
- **Tier 3 (Score 2.0-2.9):** Contact within 1 week
- **Tier 4 (Score <2.0):** Nurture/deprioritize

### 4.2 Who to Contact First and Why

#### Highest Priority Targets

1. **Owner/CEO of 20-50 unit PM company**
   - Direct decision-maker
   - Big enough to have screening volume
   - Small enough to move fast

   - Owns the screening workflow
   - Feels pain of manual verification

   - Makes own decisions
   - Values time savings
   - Often tech-forward

#### Medium Priority Targets

4. **Property Manager (titled) at growing company**
   - May not have budget authority
   - Can influence decision
   - Useful for intel gathering

5. **PM company with recent negative reviews mentioning "screening"**
   - Active pain point
   - Open to solutions
   - Research their reviews for talking points

#### Lower Priority (but still valuable)

6. **PM companies using competitors**
   - Educated buyers
   - Know they need the solution
   - May be open to switching

7. **New PM companies (< 2 years)**
   - Building their stack
   - May have limited budget
   - Long-term relationship potential
